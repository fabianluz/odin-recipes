Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of wide, flat pasta sheets, typically baked in the oven. The pasta is alternated with a rich tomato-based meat sauce, often made from ground beef or pork, along with a creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese mixture. Between the layers, shredded mozzarella and Parmesan cheese are commonly added for a gooey, savory texture. The top layer is usually topped with extra cheese to create a golden, bubbling crust once baked.

Traditional lasagna is seasoned with garlic, onions, and herbs like oregano and basil, giving it a deep, flavorful taste. While the classic version uses a meat sauce (ragù), there are many variations, including vegetarian options that substitute meat with vegetables like spinach, zucchini, or mushrooms. Lasagna is a hearty, satisfying dish often served in large portions, perfect for family dinners or gatherings.
